•Fruits are the sweet, fleshy, edible parts of a plant that typically contain seeds and are developed from the flower of the plant. They are a vital source of nutrients, including fiber, vitamins (like vitamin C), and minerals, and are enjoyed raw or cooked.

Functions of Fruits:

Seed Protection: Fruits protect seeds from harsh environmental conditions.

•Seed Dispersal: Fruits help in the dispersal of seeds, allowing plants to colonize new areas.

•Food Storage: Fruits can store food (nutrients) for the plant's future use.

•Germination: Fruits can facilitate seed germination.
